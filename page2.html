ASSET MANAGERS

Over the last decade a new kind of investor called “asset manager” have quietly grown to have enormous financial and political power. The two biggest asset managers: BlackRock and Vanguard, manage over $16tn. BlackRock alone is bigger than the economies of Germany and Japan combined. 

Broadly speaking asset managers take pools of savings or pensions and they invest them, for a fee, on our behalf.


ASSET OWNERS

Are people and institutions with money. Most asset owners entrust their money to
*asset managers*, who invest it on their behalf in order to make financial returns.

ASSET MANAGERS

Are hired by asset owners to invest their money, primarliy by buying *shares* in comapnies on capital markets.

SHARES

Shareholders own shares in a company, meaning they invest in a portion of a company and so they own part of that company. And they get a share of the profit.

Annual Meetings -Shareholder season

Publicly listed companies are required by law to have an annual meeting of their shareholders. Most companies have these meetings in the spring —mostly April and May.

At these meetings are opportunities for shareholders to weigh in on how a company does business by voting for...or against...changes.


Asset managers own signifcant  shares in the fossil fuel companies and banks that are fueling the climate crisis

That matters because owning shares gives them a say in how companies operates. 

The likes of BlackRock are so big that how they vote at annual shareholder meetings can have a material impact on a company's business plans.
