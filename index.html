THE 'almost' PLAN

At it's core the concpet that we're working on is radicaly simple. It is based on the assumption that in order to avoid ecological collapse and win the struggle for racial and economic justuce, we're going to have to prioritise work at the intersections of our movements and with a level of coordination that we've not seen or tried before.

At the centre of our concept lies the COMMUNITY CAUCUS.  Small, focused and guided group conversations aimed at establishing political alignment within a specific community and establishing a collective organising structure. 

There are 3 sets/groups of people that we believe it crucial to engage in this process:

1 - Grassroots & frontlines
2 - Broader climate movement
3 - Intersecting strugggles

Phase I

Local groups from broader national climate networks host small (7-10 people) thematic caucases. They could be focused on areas of public life that we want to see centered and supported as a priority at a local level in the future and/or discussing what local targets for action could be. 

Most important is that these caususes are held and designed alongside those actively organisng for racial and economic justice. If its only the same group of activists as usual we'll not be building teh power that we need to be building.

The broader climate movement is encouraged to hold small community assemblies at as local level as possible and from those assemblies people would be invited to signature events as part of the Glasgow Agreement, actions planned by grassroots and frontline groups, and set up local organsing hubs to start to organise around demands/thematic areas of focus emerging from the discussions.

The myth of austerity has been shattered.

After a decade of cuts to vital public services across Europe, the main argument - WE CAN'T AFFORD IT - used to justify justify massive privatisation has been torn apart. The covid19 pandemic has forced governments to use one of the most importatn powers they have to create money in order to bailout companies that would have otherwise gone bust. So far 1.2 trillion euros of new money has been created in the form of stimulus and resuce packages. For individuals and small to medium size busnessess the conditions have been strict and harsh, the money given in the form of loans. For the multinationals the handouts have largely come with no conditions whatsoever and in the form of bailoutsm with no need to be paid back.

THE GLASGOW AGREEMENT

Is building a radical coaliton of organisations, caoolecing around climate justice and civil disobedience. That is important work that we fully support and are actively engaging with. BUT the focus on infrastructure shut down will always have limited reach and over the last 10 years, while achieveing alot, it hasn't been enough to trigger the system level shift that we must aim for. 

The peoples agreement is quite different, but complimentary, to the Glasgow Agreement. Whereas the GA aims to engage groups in establishing the target of direct actions using the primary tool of civil disobedience. The Peoples agreement aims to engage communities in establishing the building blocks for collective political organising to build out the solutions and vision that is so desperately needed.
