COP26 CANCELLED

Is a headline that some have been dreaming of for a long time. It has been obvious for years that the UNFCCC process could never deliver on its' objective - of limiting warming to 1.5oC - due to the domination of corporate and financial interests. It also true that others have valued the process and the opportunity it provided to hold governments to account.

Regardless of our personal views on the utility of the UNFCCC process, COP26 has actually, for real been cancelled. This presents an important opportunity for social movements to put in place a bottom up process to challange the vested interests standing in the way head on.

Time for a  
'Peoples Agreement'

When we treat a crisis as a crisis, it turns out the impossible becomes possible.
The entire global economic system was just put on hold. We gave up comforts, intimacies and togetherness to protect those at risk. We quickly learned to change our daily habits, to self-organise mutual aid within our communities.

Imagine what we could achieve if we were to design and implement
such protective measures collectively, in order to avert ecological collapse. Prioritising only those things that, as a society now, are absolutely necessary to ensure our common survival.

Justice in sharp focus

While those of us more fortunate worked from home, many had little choice but to carry on working. 
The health workers, shelf stackers, firefighters, bus drivers put their own health at risk to provide us all with the basics. 

Then the Black Lives Matter movement in the US, triggered by the brutal murder of George Floyd, brought the violent face of racism to the fore at a global level, statistics show that, globally, people of colour are much more likely to live in poverty and therefore be impacted by the virus. 

It's the same undercurrent of racism that allows us to conveniently ignore the exploitation of black and brown bodies, the poor in the global south, from whom we are complicit in stealing the resources on which the obscene wealth of a handful of elites in the global north is built.  

DECADE ZERO

In June 2020, 45oC temperatures were recorded at multiple locations within the Arctic Circle. While we're too late to avoid what will already be sever impacts of the climate crisis, its not to late to organise, across movements, to make sure that, as pressure increases on our food, water and energy systems, we act with humality, compasion and for justice. Ensuring that everyone has access to fulfil ther basic needed and opportunity to live a fullfilling and happy life.  

If we beleive our own pandemic rhetoric, i.e. that there can be no return to normal, then that must stand for our organising  as well.

THE 'almost' PLAN

At it's core the concpet that we're working on is radicaly simple. It is based on the assumption that in order to avoid ecological collapse and win the struggle for racial and economic justuce, we're going to have to prioritise work at the intersections of our movements and with a level of coordination that we've not seen or tried before.

At the centre of our concept lies the COMMUNITY CAUCUS.  Small, focused and guided group conversations aimed at establishing political alignment within a specific community and establishing a collective organising structure. 

There are 3 sets/groups of people that we believe it crucial to engage in this process:

1 - Grassroots & frontlines
2 - Broader climate movement
3 - Intersecting strugggles

Phase I

Most important is that these caususes are held and designed alongside those actively organisng for racial and economic justice. If its only the same group of activists as usual we'll not be building teh power that we need to be building.

FAQ

 How is this different from other assembly initiatives? 

It is great that there appears to be a plurality of assemblies in the planning. We’re hearing talk coming from within XR but to date have yet to find out any more information about their plan. We’d love to be in contact to make sure our plans aren’t competing.

What about the Glasgow Agreement? 

The Glasgow Agreement is building a radical coalition of organisations, coalescing around climate justice and civil disobedience. That is important work that we fully support and are actively engaging with. BUT the focus on infrastructure shut down will always have limited reach and over the last 10 years, while achieving a lot, it hasn't been enough to trigger the system level shift that we must aim for.

The peoples agreement is quite different, but complimentary, to the Glasgow Agreement. Whereas the GA aims to engage groups in establishing the target of direct actions using the primary tool of civil disobedience. The Peoples agreement aims to engage communities in establishing the building blocks for collective political organising to build out the solutions and vision that is so desperately needed.
